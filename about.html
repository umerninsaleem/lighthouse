Meet the Founder

I’m Muhammad Umer Bin Saleem, the founder of The Lighthouse and an O Level student. This initiative began from a very personal place. I’ve lived through challenges — emotional, societal, and financial — that made me feel unseen, unheard, and limited. But within that darkness, I discovered something powerful: resilience. Rather than staying silent, I chose to speak. Rather than giving up, I chose to rise — not just for myself, but for those who couldn’t.

I realized that many students around me — especially girls — were facing similar struggles, often with even fewer opportunities. That’s when I made a promise to myself: I would be the person I once needed. And that’s exactly what The Lighthouse stands for — being a light for those still in the shadows.

Our Mission

The Lighthouse is a student-led platform created to provide free educational resources, mentorship, and support to anyone in need — with a special focus on underprivileged girls. We believe that education should not be gated by class, gender, or circumstance. Everyone deserves a chance to learn, to grow, and to step into their power.

Our mission is to build a safe, accessible, and empowering space — where learning is free, confidence is nurtured, and potential is limitless. We are not just helping students study; we are helping them believe in themselves. We are not here to escape the darkness — we are here to light the way through it.

Our Team

Our team is made up of ten passionate students from diverse academic paths — including O Level, Matriculation, and University. We're students too — just slightly more privileged — and that’s exactly why we feel responsible to uplift others. Each member contributes their own talent: whether it's creating notes, leading sessions, handling outreach, or building digital resources. We work together to ensure that no girl is left behind in her pursuit of knowledge and self-worth.
